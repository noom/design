Logo usage

There are 2 versions of the logo, big and small. It is important to use the correct file when designing with the logo. You can tell the difference between them by looking at the rows of dots. You should always be able to distinguish the dots in the logo. If you can't see the dots clearly then switch to the small logo. The big version of the Noom logo has 3 rows of dots.
The small version of the logo has only 2 rows of dots.

Colors

The color palette consists of 10 colors, each with 4 shades. Each color has a dark, main, light and lightest version of the color. The main color should be used first and the darks and lights should be used as support of the main colors.

Photography

When it comes to photography the "how" (tone) is just as important as the "what" (subject matter). The following guide will show you how to ensure everything we say with photography sounds playful, approachable, trusted and supportive.

Casual

We are casual — not dramatic. We don't go our of way to make things sexier, bolder, sleeker. Our imperfections make us approachable. Ask yourself "is the photographer trying to show off?" Casual Dramatic Casual Dramatic

Genuine

We speak through genuine moments. Not fake or forced. Real people are looking to us for real & trustworthy support. Ask yourself: "would I see this on Instagram?" Genuine moment Fake moment Real friends Fake friends Real active people Fake active person

Light-hearted

We're a glass-half-full kind of company. Take the light-hearted approach — every time. Ask yourself: "would this put a smile on my face?" Heh! Why so serious?

Layouts

The previous sections covered specific parts of our brand. Now, by combining all those color, typographic, and photographic rules you can create visual compositions that are brand-perfect.

Type always on white

Most compositions will rely on both words and imagery to convey a message. Readability will of course be our top priority. Since white is our buffer color use it to create a text-safe area. A white box gives ample room for the text and photo to co-exist. Text directly over a photo can be difficult to read. Instead, if possible, create empty (white) space in the photo. Colorizing a photo will make the text easier to read but it results in dark and gloomy compositions. Instead, place the photo in a sea of white. This allows you to properly balance the look and feel by adding touches of the new Noom colors. Sometimes a photo is simple enough to place text directly on top without colorizing.
