Logo

Primary logo

The Noom logo is a series of dots that, together, form the greater shape of the letters of Noom. The construction of the wordmark from dots is meant to symbolize that small things build up to something larger and more meaningful. This includes, among other things:
  • Daily food decisions can add up to amount to your current state of health;
  • All of us as individuals coming together to form a bigger company;
  • Users coming together in groups to create a meaningful experience when using the product.

Logo usage

There are 2 versions of the logo, big and small. It is important to use the correct file when designing with the logo. You can tell the difference between them by looking at the rows of dots. You should always be able to distinguish the dots in the logo. If you can't see the dots clearly then switch to the small logo. Minimum screen size for the 3-dot logo is 165 px in length. For any uses smaller than that, the logo should be replaced with the 2-dot version. The minimum size for the 2-dot logo is 44 px in length. Minimum print size for the 3-dot logo is 1.7 inches in length. For any uses smaller than that, the logo should be replaced with the 2-dot version. The minimum size for the 2-dot logo is .75 inches in length. The big version of the Noom logo has 3 rows of dots.
The small version of the logo has only 2 rows of dots.

Clear space

Always maintain clear space around the Noom logo to protect from distracting graphics or typography. Measure clear space by the height and width of the letter “o” of the respective logo being used.

Colors

The color palette consists of 18 colors, each with 6 shades. The various shades are defined numerically with 1 being the lightest and 6 being the darkest.

The primary color palette consists of 10 colors, each with 4 shades. It is meant to be used for design of all in app product features. For specific details on the use of each color within the app, see the Interface Guidelines

Type sizing

Try to use the right font for the right size. Notice how each headline seems to have the same thickness as the rest, even though they are different sizes. Light Regular Medium Bold

Type tone

Never use all-caps. We don't shout. We speak. Better Shouty

Type Display

Most compositions will rely on both words and imagery to convey a message. Readability will of course be our top priority. Since white is our buffer color use it to create a text-safe area. A white box gives ample room for the text and photo to co-exist. Text directly over a photo can be difficult to read. Instead, if possible, create empty (white) space in the photo. Colorizing a photo will make the text easier to read but it results in dark and gloomy compositions. Place the photo in a sea of white. This allows you to properly balance the look and feel by adding touches of the new Noom colors. Sometimes a photo is simple enough to place text directly on top without colorizing.

Photography

When it comes to photography the "how" (tone) is just as important as the "what" (subject matter). The following guide will show you how to ensure everything we say with photography feels playful, approachable, trusted and supportive.

Casual

We are casual — not dramatic. We don't go our of way to make things sexier, bolder, sleeker. Our imperfections make us approachable. Ask yourself "is the photographer trying to show off?" Casual Dramatic Casual Dramatic Casual Dramatic

Genuine

We speak through genuine moments. Not fake or forced. Real people are looking to us for real & trustworthy support. Ask yourself: "would I see this on Instagram?"
