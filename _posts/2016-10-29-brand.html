Brand Guide

Noom’s mission is simple: Create products that help people lead healthier lives. Our programs help users develop the skills they need to develop and sustain a healthy lifestyle long-term. We build structured programs with human coaches that tackle pre-chronic and chronic conditions. This guide outlines the values, assets and usage of the Noom brand. These guidelines should be followed in all media in which the Noom brand is present. With our cutting-edge technology and expert coaches, we aim to keep our users on track throughout their journeys and prepare them for a lifetime of good health. We hope that you enjoy the process of helping us improve our product while enhancing the user’s journey. If you have any questions or comments in regards to the branding guidelines, please contact {{ site.email }}.

Values

What are our core values?
  • We make users happy
    From our beautiful user interface to our spectacular coaches, we stive to make users happy and eager to use our product. Providing an enjoyable user experience helps them in their journey to lead a healthier lifestyle.
  • We take pride in our product
    Noomers are passionate about the work they do and take great pride in our product.
  • We make healthy living fun
    To help our users reach their health goals, we make healthy living fun with interactive challenges and vibrant illustrations.
  • We educate our users
    We provide our users with the knowledge they need to lead healthy and enjoyable lives.
  • We are empathetic
    Noom knows the frustrations that come with lifestyle change. Our empathy allows us to better understand what users need to be successful.

Our Tone

    We are:

  • Friendly
  • Intelligent
  • Playful
  • Trustworthy
  • Approachable
  • Supportive
  • Passionate

    We are not:

  • Competitive
  • Uptight
  • Unprofessional
  • Timid
  • Inconsistent
  • Dishonest
  • Aggressive
  • We would say something like this:
    Our coaches understand that losing weight is just as much a mental game as a physical one. Making changes can cause fear, anxiety, and stress, but they will be there for you through it all. They’ll be more than a coach, they’ll be a partner and they will help motivate and keep you going when the going gets tough.

    Our coaches understand that losing weight is hard and it will always be that way. There’s anxiety about all aspects of weight loss. That’s why they’ll be more than a coach, but only if you can meet them halfway. They’ll be there for you and try to motivate you when you need it.

Primary logo

The Noom logo is a series of dots that, together, form the greater shape of the letters of Noom. The construction of the wordmark from dots is meant to symbolize that small things build up to something larger and more meaningful. This includes, among other things:
Daily food decisions can add up to amount to your current state of health;
All of us as individuals coming together to form a bigger company;
Users coming together in groups to create a meaningful experience when using the product.

Logo usage

There are 2 versions of the logo, big and small. It is important to use the correct file when designing with the logo. You can tell the difference between them by looking at the rows of dots. You should always be able to distinguish the dots in the logo. If you can't see the dots clearly then switch to the small logo. Minimum screen size for the 3-dot logo is 165 px in length. For any uses smaller than that, the logo should be replaced with the 2-dot version. The minimum size for the 2-dot logo is 44 px in length. Minimum print size for the 3-dot logo is 1.7 inches in length. For any uses smaller than that, the logo should be replaced with the 2-dot version. The minimum size for the 2-dot logo is .75 inches in length.

The big version of the Noom logo has 3 rows of dots.

The small version of the logo has only 2 rows of dots.

Clear space

Always maintain clear space around the Noom logo to protect from distracting graphics or typography. Measure clear space by the height and width of the letter “o” of the respective logo being used.

Type Display

Most compositions will rely on both words and imagery to convey a message. Readability will of course be our top priority. Since white is our buffer color use it to create a text-safe area. A white box gives ample room for the text and photo to co-exist. Text directly over a photo can be difficult to read. Instead, if possible, create empty (white) space in the photo. Colorizing a photo will make the text easier to read but it results in dark and gloomy compositions. Place the photo in a sea of white. This allows you to properly balance the look and feel by adding touches of the new Noom colors. Sometimes a photo is simple enough to place text directly on top without colorizing.

Casual

We are casual — not dramatic. We don't go our of way to make things sexier, bolder, sleeker. Our imperfections make us approachable. Ask yourself "is the photographer trying to show off?" Casual Dramatic Casual Dramatic Casual Dramatic
